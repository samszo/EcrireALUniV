1 Ouvrages de référence

1.1 Méthodologie générale

Beaud, M. (2006). L’art de la thèse : Comment préparer et rédiger un mémoire de master, une thèse de doctorat ou tout autre travail universitaire à l’ère du Net. Paris : La Découverte.

Booth, W. C., Colomb, G. G., & Williams, J. M. (2016). The Craft of Research (4th ed.). Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

Eco, U. (2016). Comment écrire sa thèse. Paris : Flammarion. (Ouvrage original publié en 1977).

1.2 Écriture académique

Delcambre, I., & Lahanier-Reuter, D. (Éds.). (2012). Littéracies universitaires : nouvelles perspectives. Villeneuve d’Ascq : Presses Universitaires du Septentrion.

Pollet, M.-C. (2012). De la maîtrise du français aux littéracies dans l’enseignement supérieur. Namur : Presses universitaires de Namur.

Reuter, Y. (2004). Analyser les problèmes de l’écriture de recherche en formation. Pratiques, 121-122, 9-27.

1.3 Style et rédaction

Strunk, W., & White, E. B. (2000). The Elements of Style (4th ed.). New York: Longman.

Zinsser, W. (2006). On Writing Well: The Classic Guide to Writing Nonfiction (30th Anniversary ed.). New York: HarperCollins.

2 Articles scientifiques

2.1 Didactique de l’écriture

Beaufort, A. (2007). College Writing and Beyond: A New Framework for University Writing Instruction. Logan: Utah State University Press.

Carlino, P. (2013). Alfabetización académica diez años después. Revista Mexicana de Investigación Educativa, 18(57), 355-381.

Delcambre, I., & Donahue, C. (2012). Academic writing activity: Student writing in transition. In M. Castelló & C. Donahue (Eds.), University Writing: Selves and Texts in Academic Societies (pp. 129-149). Bingley: Emerald.

2.2 Processus d’écriture

Flower, L., & Hayes, J. R. (1981). A Cognitive Process Theory of Writing. College Composition and Communication, 32(4), 365-387.

Hayes, J. R. (2012). Modeling and Remodeling Writing. Written Communication, 29(3), 369-388.

2.3 Littéracies universitaires

Barton, D., & Hamilton, M. (2000). Literacy Practices. In D. Barton, M. Hamilton, & R. Ivanič (Eds.), Situated Literacies: Reading and Writing in Context (pp. 7-15). London: Routledge.

Lea, M. R., & Street, B. V. (1998). Student Writing in Higher Education: An Academic Literacies Approach. Studies in Higher Education, 23(2), 157-172.

3 Manuels de citation

3.1 APA Style

American Psychological Association. (2020). Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (7th ed.). Washington, DC: American Psychological Association.

3.2 MLA Style

Modern Language Association. (2021). MLA Handbook (9th ed.). New York: Modern Language Association of America.

3.3 Chicago Style

The University of Chicago Press. (2017). The Chicago Manual of Style (17th ed.). Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

Turabian, K. L. (2018). A Manual for Writers of Research Papers, Theses, and Dissertations (9th ed.). Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

5 Ouvrages sur la pensée critique

Bowell, T., & Kemp, G. (2015). Critical Thinking: A Concise Guide (4th ed.). London: Routledge.

Fisher, A. (2011). Critical Thinking: An Introduction (2nd ed.). Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.

Paul, R., & Elder, L. (2019). The Miniature Guide to Critical Thinking Concepts and Tools (8th ed.). Lanham: Rowman & Littlefield.

6 Ouvrages disciplinaires

6.1 Sciences sociales

Becker, H. S. (2020). Writing for Social Scientists: How to Start and Finish Your Thesis, Book, or Article (3rd ed.). Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

Guibert, J., & Jumel, G. (2016). Méthodologie des pratiques de terrain en sciences humaines et sociales. Paris : Armand Colin.

6.2 Sciences exactes

Alley, M. (2018). The Craft of Scientific Writing (4th ed.). New York: Springer.

Hofmann, A. H. (2019). Scientific Writing and Communication: Papers, Proposals, and Presentations (4th ed.). Oxford: Oxford University Press.

6.3 Littérature et humanités

Culler, J. (2011). Literary Theory: A Very Short Introduction (2nd ed.). Oxford: Oxford University Press.

Rabatel, A. (2012). Positions, positionnements et postures de l’énonciateur. Travaux neuchâtelois de linguistique, 56, 23-42.

7 Gestion du temps et productivité

Allen, D. (2015). Getting Things Done: The Art of Stress-Free Productivity. New York: Penguin Books.

Newport, C. (2016). Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World. New York: Grand Central Publishing.

Silvia, P. J. (2019). How to Write a Lot: A Practical Guide to Productive Academic Writing (2nd ed.). Washington, DC: American Psychological Association.

8 Aspects psychologiques

Boice, R. (1990). Professors as Writers: A Self-Help Guide to Productive Writing. Stillwater: New Forums Press.

Bolker, J. (1998). Writing Your Dissertation in Fifteen Minutes a Day: A Guide to Starting, Revising, and Finishing Your Doctoral Thesis. New York: Henry Holt.

9 Éthique et intégrité académique

Bretag, T. (Ed.). (2016). Handbook of Academic Integrity. Singapore: Springer.

Stern, J. E., & Elliott, D. (1997). The Ethics of Scientific Research: A Guidebook for Course Development. Hanover: University Press of New England.
